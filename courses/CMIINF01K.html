Cursusdoelen
- The student gets insight in the possibilities and added value of information display. - The student learns how to disclose complex information to an audience and to decision makers, by formulating the right question. - The student learns how to transform quantitative information into easy-to-understand images.
Inhoud
Entry level: Basic knowledge of information graphs (line graph, bar graph, pie charts).
Teaching methods:
Lectures
Practice / group coaching
Assignments
Final presentation or exhibition of deliverables
Contents: Information from research, files or databases is regularly expressed in a dull way. But it is possible to do that in a beautiful way that also reveals extra insights!
Information can be designed in such a way that it is pleasurable to look at and also delivers unique insights in complex matter. An intelligent, visual design is able to express complex issues in a simple way, evolving in unexpected insights for improved decision making.
You will learn how information can be disclosed in other forms than dull graphs only. These other forms very from narrative illustrations to artistic interpretations. You will work on disclosing a dataset to others with easy-to-understand images. This has less to do with statistics but very much to do with asking the right question: what do I want to know and how can I visually express the answers to that?
Type of exam: Attendance is compulsory.
Students work individually and in groups on (homework)assignments. Partial grades are weighted into a final average grade.
De beoordelingscriteria van de opdrachten staan in de modulewijzer beschreven zodat voor een individuele student helder is waaraan hij moet voldoen.
Literature: Reader with information from the books Data Flow (Klanten, et al.) and Envisioning Information (Tufte).
Remarks: De module is geschikt voor alle opleidingen die zich bezig houden met het ontsluiten en inichtelijk maken van kwantitatieve data en ontwikkelt de competentie op het gebied van het praktijkgericht onderzoek.
