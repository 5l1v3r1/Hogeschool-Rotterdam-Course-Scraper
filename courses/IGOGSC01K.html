Cursusdoelen
The world changes every day. The world becomes more international, travelling becomes more easy. Even when it comes to the production of goods, it is more often about goods not produced in The Netherlands. We eat apples coming from Argentina, when our own apples are not available. Our smartphones are made in China. This is called globalization of the economy. These changes are on such a worldwide scale that they change the complete world order. China is prominent as the biggest exporter in the world and the world's second economy. These changes affect The Netherlands too.  Trade flows not necessarily always through the Rotterdam Port and Europe. This course is all about these changes. Upcoming economies like Brazil, India, Russia and of course China and their connection with The Netherlands are all topics.

· The student can indicate what the BRICS countries are and what their trade relationship with the Netherlands is.
· The student can indicate what globalization is, its history and its impacts on the global supply chains.
· The student can indicate developments and changes in the global supply chains and the consequences for the port of Rotterdam.
· The student can explain why Eastern Europe is going to play a more important role in the production of electronics.
· The student is able to indicate the different transport options between Asia and Europe.
· The student is able to stay up to date regarding developments in the field of global supply chains.
Inhoud
Verantwoording: 
Shifting global flows of goods, the emergence of new economies and the associated implications for flows of goods passing through the port of Rotterdam, and the consequences for the Netherlands as a whole.
 
Inhoud:
Developments and changes in the global flow of goods and supply chains are mapped, as well as the consequences for the Netherlands and the Port of Rotterdam in particular. Worldwide trade flows are shown. The concept of globalization and its history are explained. The BRICS countries, which countries are these, and what is taking place in terms of economic activities. The impact on the flow of goods to and from Europe. How these countries organize their exports to Europe. Further focus on China as the biggest (trade) partner of Europe. The specific changes that are already visible in Europe. Chinese brands, which are already present on the European market. Special attention for Parchim International Airport in Germany and for the Port of Piraeus in Greece, both of which are developed by Chinese parties. The relations with the BRICS countries are also discussed.
 
Opmerkingen:
Open to all students who want to look beyond their own field (and part of the exchange programme Logistics Management)
