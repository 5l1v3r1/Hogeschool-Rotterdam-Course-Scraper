Cursusdoelen
Do you feel like increasing your creativity while gaining practical experience? Experience you can implement both in and
outside your classroom? Why not subscribe to the workshop 'Tips and tricks for making a short film.
Inhoud
responsibility: 
 
This course responds to everyday's trend (life is becoming increasingly more visual). She supports the student in
achieving study success ; the student acquires skills that are useful in, as well as outside, the classroom. The student
benefits from learning to tell a story not just in a textual, but first and foremost in a visual form. This easily fits into shifts in
today's society.
 
 At the end of the course the student:
- has gained basic filmmaking knowledge
- Will be better at using the medium film for study purposes Kan student het medium film (beter) inzetten voor
onderwijsdoeleinden
- Will be able to distinguish fact and fiction, observation and interpretation Kan student onderscheid maken tussen
feit en fictie, waarneming en interpretatie
- Will be able to work with film production tools Kan student omgaan met tools voor bewerken van filmpjes
 
Startlevel: 
 
This course will be open to students from each year. No prior knowledge is required. The student has to have access
though to a filmmaking device, whether it's a mobile phone/ smartphone, a tablet a digital camcorder or a (digital)
camera. 
 
Context: 
 
The following subjects will be addressed:
- Introduction to the use of film for educational purposes.
- The distinct phases of filmmaking; where possible illustrated by way of theory and paying attention to useful (film
production)tools:
Phase 1: Pre production (from concept to script)
Phase 2: Production (the actual filming)
Phase 3: Post production (from editing to screening)
 
The working methods are:
- Transfer of film theory
- Film making (working on a short film in groups of two students)
- Evaluating each other's work as well as offering feedback
 
Examination type
 
Film making
In week 9 and 10 every group of 2 has to present a film to the class. 
This film will be judged by way of the following criteria:
 
- Has it been developed in accordance to the three phases of the production process (pre production, production and
post production) and can this be proven by a required log book, describing each phase as well as containing the
corresponding products?
- To what extent does the end product connect to the content and form of the film: does the message get through to the
viewer?
- Is the message the film carries, shaped in an adequate way?
- Does the film contain creative and original elements?
