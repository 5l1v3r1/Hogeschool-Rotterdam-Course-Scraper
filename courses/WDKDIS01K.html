Cursusdoelen
Drawing techniques continue to exist in all professional practices where objects and ideas need to be visualized and sketched. Drawing can be used in that proces for documenting or visualizing reality, but also as design- or presentation methodology that one can use to sketch, develop or present an idea. The course provides different techniques and methods that enables students to acquire drawing and visualizing skills. This elective is part of the theme Creative Professions.
Inhoud
Entry level:
This course is open for all first- and second year students at Hogeschool Rotterdam.
Learning target:
- The student is able to carry out a visual research.
- The student shows affinity to drawing and uses his own visual research for inspiration
- The student applies drawing techniques and shows insight into different ways in which images can create experiences and communicate meaning.
- The student sufficiently masters drawing skills and techniques
- The student is capable of presenting his work in an adequate and convincing manner.
- The student can document and archive visual works.
- The student can analyse his own work in relation to the field of fine art and visual culture, as well as to the work of fellow students.
- The student can deal with critique and give constructive feedback
Content:
The module “drawing is seeing” is part of de elective programs of the University of Rotterdam in order to give students the opportunity to broader the drawing, observing and analysing skills and put them on a higher level. Although the manual drawing skills are being overtaken by the computer based design programs, the craft of using charcoal and pencil is still the most direct way of registration the reality. Enhancing the sketching skills improves your ability to visual communication and the design process itself. It gives you a method of visualizing concepts and expressing creativity.Because of the time intensity of the work it forces the drawer to observe and analyse the object which is visualized. It is not about making a photographical copy, but it gives the opportunity to interpret and express. In a program of practice based lessons, different techniques and approaches are being offered. Conceptual drawing, (nude) model drawing, perspective drawing, idea-registration.
Method:
Atelier.
Assessment:
Students work on assignments. Every week work is presented to see and learn from each other. Students create a portfolio with the results, which towards the end is being judged on quality and relevance ass well as the attitude and commitment of the student during the whole course. It is an intensive course but also fun to.
